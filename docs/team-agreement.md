## Team Values

* Every team member must follow team values throughout the project, as they guide how we work together and ensure accountability. If this expectation is not met, we will reach out to the specific team member(s) to better understand why and work together to ensure it is. We will use our Slack channel and meetings to address our concerns.  
* Team members should and are required to attend regular class sessions and meetings scheduled outside of class (scheduled through Slack channel, meeting either in person or through Slack huddle) with their expected work completed. If a team member must be absent from a team meeting, they must notify the team beforehand. Tasks with tentative team deadlines will be discussed during team meetings. Team members should be able to rely on each other to complete our tasks on time, so we can progress in a timely manner.   
* The team will strive to foster a welcoming and inclusive environment where members feel comfortable and respected when sharing ideas, asking questions, and seeking help.  
* All team members will aim to complete their respective tasks for each iteration by the day before the class deadline. This ensures that there is adequate time for further debugging, solving git conflicts, and other problems that may occur before the submission.  
* Everyone is expected to support one another and take responsibility for the overall quality of all project deliverables. While there may be different opinions on how to achieve success within the team, members should be open to compromise and understand that it is sometimes more important to find a working solution than to be “right”. If a decision is hard to make, we can also reach out to the professor to seek possible help/suggestions. Regardless, we will aim to resolve any problems at the earliest convenience.

## Team Communication 

* Team members will meet with their assigned advisor weekly, and with each other every other day. Usually, team members will meet some time between 10:00 am \- 12:00 pm on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.   
* Team members are expected to participate in meetings. If a team member misses or is late to a meeting, they are required to reach out to the rest of the team in advance (or at their earliest convenience) to provide notice, and to follow up afterwards to ensure there are no pending tasks. Last-minute notices of a team member’s absence are occasionally understandable, but consistent last-minute absences are unacceptable.   
* At the beginning of each iteration, an availability schedule will be provided. Team members will be available for deployment/delivery outside regular business hours or on weekends.   
* Slack will be the primary communication tool, while texting via cell phone will be used for urgent correspondence  
* A response on Slack is expected within 12 hours  
  * Use the thumbs-up reaction to indicate agreement or acknowledgement  
  * Engage in a conversation in a dedicated thread for questions, comments, objections, etc.  
  * Use the check mark emoji to show that action has taken in response to a thread.  
  * Use the eyes emojis to indicate that you'll look into the matter later.  
* Any issues that may prevent the completion of assigned tasks should be proactively communicated
